                    <h1>Food is too salty</h1>
                    <ul>
                        <li>Dilution: Add more of the main ingredients to balance out the salt. For example, if it's a soup or stew, you can add more water, broth, or unsalted ingredients like vegetables or meat.</li>
                        <li>Potatoes: Adding peeled, raw potatoes to your dish can absorb some of the salt. Let them simmer in the dish for about 15-20 minutes, then remove them.</li>
                        <li>Acidic Ingredients: Adding a splash of vinegar, lemon juice, or tomatoes can help counteract the saltiness.</li>
                        <li>Dairy: Stirring in some milk, cream, or unsalted yogurt can mellow out the saltiness.</li>
                        <li>Sugar: A small amount of sugar or honey can balance out the saltiness. Be careful not to add too much, though.</li>
                        <li>Bread: For soups or stews, adding a piece of bread and letting it soak can help absorb the salt. Remove the bread before serving.</li>
                    </ul>

                    <h1>Dough for bread or cakes doesn't rise</h1>
                    <ul>
                        <li>Correct Type of Flour: Make sure you are using the right type of flour for your cake or bread. Cake flour is typically used for cakes, while bread flour is better for bread.</li>
                        <li>Proper Ingredient Ratios: Follow the recipe accurately, especially the ratios of flour, sugar, butter, and eggs. Cream the sugar and butter until they are light and fluffy before adding the eggs.</li>
                        <li>Correct Oven Temperature: An oven that is too hot or too cold can cause issues. Use an oven thermometer to ensure the right temperature.</li>
                        <li>Proper Baking Time: Don't bake for too long or too short a time. Follow the recommended baking time in the recipe and check for doneness with a toothpick or skewer.</li>
                        <li>Proper Mixing Technique: Avoid overmixing or undermixing the batter. Overmixing can cause too much gluten to form, making the cake or bread tough.</li>
                        <li>Room Temperature Ingredients: Ensure all ingredients, especially butter and eggs, are at room temperature before you start mixing.</li>
                    </ul>

                    <h1>The cooking sauce is too thin</h1>
                    <ol>
                        <li>Cornstarch Slurry: Mix 1-2 tablespoons of cornstarch with an equal amount of cold water to form a slurry. Stir it into the sauce and let it simmer until it thickens.</li>
                        <li>Flour and Butter Paste (Beurre Manié): Knead together equal parts of softened butter and flour until you have a smooth paste. Stir small amounts into the sauce and cook until it thickens.</li>
                        <li>Reduce the Sauce: Simmer the sauce over low to medium heat to allow excess liquid to evaporate. Stir frequently to prevent burning and to ensure even thickening.</li>
                        <li>Add Pureed Vegetables: Blend cooked vegetables like potatoes, carrots, or tomatoes and stir the puree into the sauce. This will not only thicken the sauce but also enhance its flavor.</li>
                        <li>Add Cream or Coconut Milk: For creamy sauces, adding a bit of heavy cream or coconut milk can thicken the consistency and add richness.</li>
                        <li>Use Egg Yolks: For certain sauces, you can whisk in beaten egg yolks. Temper the egg yolks with a bit of hot sauce before adding them to prevent curdling.</li>    
                    </ol>
                </div>

                    <img src="dagingtakempuk.jpg" alt="Meat not tender">
                    <h1>The meat is not tender</h1>
                    <ol>
                        <li>Cook at a Lower Temperature: Sometimes, slow and low cooking can help break down the tough fibers in the meat. Consider lowering the heat and cooking for a longer period.</li>
                        <li>Add Acidic Ingredients: Ingredients like vinegar, lemon juice, or tomatoes can help break down the tough fibers. Try adding a bit to your cooking liquid.</li>
                        <li>Use a Meat Tenderizer: Physically tenderizing the meat with a mallet can break down tough muscle fibers.</li>
                        <li>Marinate: Marinating meat in acidic ingredients (like buttermilk, yogurt, or a vinegar-based marinade) can also help tenderize it. Allow it to marinate for several hours or overnight.</li>
                        <li>Braising: This method involves cooking meat slowly in a covered pot with some liquid. The steam and slow cooking process helps make the meat tender.</li>
                        <li>Pressure Cooker: If you have a pressure cooker, it can greatly reduce cooking time while making the meat tender.</li>
                    </ol>
